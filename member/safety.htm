  • Safety Committee Mission

    The Safety Committee was formed in 2019 to provide increased focus on Safety in how the club and its members operate, and to provide a more formal mechanism by which safety issues may be addressed. Toward that end the following high level objectives were set:

    1. - Substantially improve our Safety Culture Index.
    2. - Substantially improve the attitudes and behavior that could lead a change from BUREAUCRATIC SAFETY CULTURE to a POSITIVE SAFETY CULTURE.
    3. - Implement an Aviation Safety Management Program to address goals.

    Specific goals are set out in the OFC Safety Policy and 2021 Objectives document.

    The Safety Committee can be contacted using ofc-safety@oxfordflyingclub.com.

  • Proficiency Time and Flight Checks

    Proficiency Time, sometimes referred to as Bank Time, is an important feature of our Club Safety Culture. The goal of Proficiency Time is to ensure that Club members maintain flight currency by encouraging a minimal amount of recent flight experience. If a member loses money through the loss of prepaid Proficiency Time, he/she is more likely to consider whether his/her focus and interests are consistent with a high standard of safety, and make appropriate changes.

    Our Club also requires Flight Checks beyond those mandated by the FAA. Annual Insurance Checks with an Instructor are required for each type of Club aircraft to be flown, and an annual Instrument Proficiency Check is required to fly Club aircraft under IFR in IMC or VMC conditions. These Flight Checks help maintain and improve skills while ensuring that Club Safety Standards are being observed.

    See the Training and Operations Manual and AIC and IPC templates available on the documents page for more details.

  • OFC, Hazard / Incident Reporting System

    To raise safety awareness, establish an accurate central record, and provide an opportunity for members to learn from the experience of others, our Club has established a process whereby members can report, anonymously if desired, items related to safety hazards. Members are encouraged to share information by filling out a form and submitting it to a member of the Safety Committee or Board of Directors. Every effort will be made to maintain confidentiality unless otherwise desired. Remember that we all makes mistakes but we can improve by reviewing the mistakes of others.

    To simplify the reporting of safety-related incidents the previously used HIR and FOIR forms have been combined and replaced by a one-page Confidential Safety Report form. To report an incident please fill out the CSR form and submit it to the Safety Officer or another member of the Safety Committee. An initial phone call may be apropriate to maintain confidentiality.
